Wrist Paddle — FAQ

The paddle moves the wrong way / controls feel reversed.
The game auto-detects which wrist you wear your watch on, but if it guessed wrong: open the Wrist Paddle app on your iPhone → Settings → turn on "Set wrist manually" → pick your wrist. Fixed instantly, no restart needed.
The game keeps pausing and saying HOLD FLAT.
That's the game telling you your watch has tipped away from the position you started in. Wrist Paddle calibrates to wherever your arm is resting the moment you tap Start — so rest your forearm somewhere comfortable first, then tap Start, and keep your arm roughly there while you play. Only your wrist should move. If it keeps happening, quit to the menu and tap Start again from your actual comfortable position.
There's no sound.
Three checks, in order: (1) iPhone app → Settings → "Mute in-game sound" should be OFF; (2) your watch isn't in Silent Mode or Theater Mode; (3) if you're wearing Bluetooth headphones, sound routes there.
The paddle feels too twitchy / too sluggish.
Adjust sensitivity — it's on both devices: on the Watch, tap "Sensitivity" on the game's home screen; on the iPhone, it's in the Settings tab. Changes apply instantly, even mid-session.
I bought Tier 2/3 but nothing unlocked.
Give it a few seconds with both apps open — purchases sync from phone to watch. Still nothing? iPhone app → Shop → "Restore Purchases." If that fails, confirm you're signed into the same Apple ID that made the purchase.
My score didn't show up on my phone / the leaderboard.
Scores sync when a game ends. If your phone wasn't nearby, the score is queued and arrives when both devices are next together — it isn't lost. Note: Zen Mode scores intentionally don't count toward your best score, rank, or leaderboard — Zen is pressure-free practice by design.
Does this drain my battery?
The motion sensors only run during actual gameplay and the tutorial — never on menus or in the background.
Can I play without the wrist controls?
Yes — iPhone app → Settings → "Use Digital Crown instead of wrist motion." The paddle then follows the crown, no arm position required.
